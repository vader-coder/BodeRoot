Define \[H(s)=C\frac{N(s)}{D(s)}\]





\[H(s)=C\frac{s^2 + 5s + 6}{s^5 + 4s^4 + 7s^3 + 6s^2 + 2s}\]

We start with the transfer function (note: the process here follows that on the second page of the file BodeRules.pdf):

H(s)=Cs2+5s+6s5+4s4+7s3+6s2+2sH(s)=Cs2+5s+6s5+4s4+7s3+6s2+2s

We rewrite it by factoring into real poles & zeros, complex poles & zeros and poles & zeros at the origin.

H(s)=C(1s)(s+ωz1)(s+ωz2)(s+ωp1)2(s2+2ζp1ω0p1s+ω20p1)H(s)=C(1s)(s+ωz1)(s+ωz2)(s+ωp1)2(s2+2ζp1ω0p1s+ω0p12)

With:

  • Constant: C=10
  • A real pole at s=-1.00, of muliplicity 2.
    This is the (s+ωp1)2(s+ωp1)2 term in the denominator, with ωp1ωp1=1.
  • A real zero at s=-2.00.
    This is the (s+ωz1)(s+ωz1) term in the numerator, with ωz1ωz1=2.
  • A real zero at s=-3.00.
    This is the (s+ωz2)(s+ωz2) term in the numerator, with ωz2ωz2=3.
  • Complex poles, at s = -1.00 ± 1.00j.
    This is the (s2+2ζp1ω0p1s+ω20p1)(s2+2ζp1ω0p1s+ω0p12) term in the denominator, with ω0p1ω0p1=1.41, ζp1ζp1=0.707.
  • A pole at the origin.

Next we write all the poles and zeros is our standard form.

H(s)=Cωz1ωz2ω2p1ω20p1(1s)(1+s/ωz1)(1+s/ωz2)(1+s/ωp1)2(1+2ζp1(s/ω0p1)+(s/ω20p1))H(s)=Cωz1ωz2ωp12ω0p12(1s)(1+s/ωz1)(1+s/ωz2)(1+s/ωp1)2(1+2ζp1(s/ω0p1)+(s/ω0p12))

Rewrite the constant:

K=Cωz1ωz2ω2p1ω20p1=30.0=29.6dBK=Cωz1ωz2ωp12ω0p12=30.0=29.6dB

So

H(s)=K(1s)(1+s/ωz1)(1+s/ωz2)(1+s/ωp1)2(1+2ζp1(s/ω0p1)+(s/ω20p1))H(s)=K(1s)(1+s/ωz1)(1+s/ωz2)(1+s/ωp1)2(1+2ζp1(s/ω0p1)+(s/ω0p12))

Now the transfer function is in the form we need to apply our rules to draw the Bode plot.
